Well so far as I can see, speaking from what little experience that I posess, version control is pretty nifty! It lets you revise your code in a controlled manner. I would have to say that out of any pick, right now, I'd choose GitHub's form of version control. This form makes it really easy to see what files go into what you are making. Fangirling aside, GitHub's version control creates this sense of security(the community) and power(the licensing) both of which I love! What is the deal with "inline" and "inline-block"?

11/28/15

Wait, what? Why even compare the two, they are the same thing right? At first glance it would be easy to assume such things. The truth is that these two displays act similarily but they have a few differences. To start off, let's explain specifically what the two really are. Starting with inline.

Inline: This display option lets the user display the element they want "inline" with the flow of the page versus being stacked on top of one another.
